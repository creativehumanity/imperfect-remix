# Research Findings – Pilot Study
## Imperfect Remix · Paper 05: "The Human Residue"
### Creative Humanity AI Hub · June 2026

---

This document summarises early findings from a pilot session of the Imperfect Remix framework.
Dataset: 3 tracks across 3 groups (n=1 per group).
Full findings will be published in Working Paper 05: *"The Human Residue"* (forthcoming).

---

## Pilot Dataset

| Track | Group | Total Score |
|---|---|---|
| Here Comes the Sun – The Beatles | 100% Human | 54/100 |
| On My Way – Hannah Rose, Marco Nieschka, Robbie Monaghan | Human + AI | 72/100 |
| My Old Friend – Suno | 100% AI | 66/100 |

---

## Key Finding: Silence & Space

Silence & Space is the dimension most resistant to AI simulation.
Scores follow the expected order of human involvement exactly:

| Group | Silence & Space Score |
|---|---|
| 100% Human | 53 |
| Human + AI | 41 |
| 100% AI | 32 |

> *"Silence requires an artist who understands that absence carries meaning,*
> *and who is willing to risk the audience's discomfort with that absence.*
> *This is a volitional act. It is not reproduced by predicting the next token."*
>
> – Imperfect Remix Research Report, June 2026

---

## Structural Asymmetry

41-point gap between strongest and weakest dimension across all tracks:

- **Strongest:** Micro-Timing (avg 83/100) – surface complexity, learnable by AI
- **Weakest:** Silence & Space (avg 42/100) – intentional absence, not yet simulable

This gap constitutes the empirical core of Paper 05's central thesis:
AI systems can simulate the *shape* of human imperfection
while remaining structurally incapable of simulating the *intention* behind it.

---

## Paradoxical Finding

The 100% Human track scored lowest overall (54/100).
This does not indicate that AI collaboration produces more human music.
It reveals that the framework measures the *legibility* of human residue –
a property that depends on how accessible the human process is to the scoring instruments,
not solely on authorship.

Deeply embedded, historically situated humanity (The Beatles, 1969)
resists measurement differently than contemporary hybrid production.
This is a methodological finding, not a value judgement.
